How it Works

Nuclear energy is considered to be a green energy source, but not renewable as it requires one key component, uranium 235. Nuclear power plants take this uranium and split it apart atom by atom. This releases massive amounts of energy as heat, which turns water into steam. The steam is used to turn the reactor's turbines to generate electricity. However, there is an unfortunate byproduct of this process. Nuclear power plants leave behind radioactive waste which require very careful procedures to keep safe. And this waste can stay dangerous for up to 1000 years. There is a second theoretical type of nuclear reactor which utilizes nuclear fusion. This process would be clean and renewable without the dangerous waste, but the technology for it is still years away.
